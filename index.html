10.06.14

Day 1: London - Madrid

First morning. Unceremoniously awoken by K’s Italians, who, it turns out, were flying out this morning. Noise was heroically loud, was like a bad impression of somebody trying to be quiet but failing absolutely for comic effect. It is now 07:30, will be leaving soon. K’s place is 2 floors up opposite the Regina Sophia. Pokey. Slept on a sofa about 2ft too small and so feeling a little shrunken and worse for wear. Arrived yesterday at lunchtime after an early flight. Have eaten only potatoes, eggs and bread for the last 24 hours.

Yesterday saw a little of the city, sat in parks and bars, spoke to K’s friends of cycling, of Europe and art. We are a full contingent now Josh having arrived last night and D coming over with me. The city is big and small. Hiding from itself. On hills and without respite. The architecture seems homogenous except startling modernist drops. Like Regina Sophia and some other cultural buildings. Me and D visited this gallery for a few hours last night, its free from 7-9. Saw Miro, Picasso, Dali, Gonzales, other Spanish modernists. Also saw the beginning of a exhibition on playgrounds.

11.06.14

Day 2: Madrid - Granada

Awoken on day 2 (morning 2) in Granada. Staying in Albaicín a mini-Marrakesh in the north of the city in the shadow of the Alhambra. Josh and D have just returned with pastries and K is in the shower.

Yesterday was overburdened. Was a huge and ripe day. Began early and after some car/sat nav issues we got to the airport for insurance and then to our first stop in Toledo. Parking by the bull ring we walking up into the old city climbing and trying to get lost in its passages and alleyways. The cathedral here was stunning crammed in but splendid. The Jewish quarter was a relief from the gawd of much of the lower slopes and from here we could take in the whole city. Brought some soft fruit and a sandwich and drove on.

Slept and read for the next few hours before arriving in Jean. This town was an unexpected delight. Again, the old town and the cathedral dominated the visit. We climbed to the highest point in the town to take in the vista. Jean was sleepy but welcoming. We made up a possible future life in Jean, of writing and falling in love. Had some bar tapas and from here back on the road.

The final stop and resting point was Granada. Parking on the hill and walking into town before scoffing a falafel and heading back up to the palace for our 10:00pm spot, was all we had time for, considering everything else we’d packed into the day. The palace was stunning, indescribable. The non-figurative art was more transcendent more meditative and holy that any of the catholic iconography we’d seen throughout the day. The day finished in a fusion palace with a beer and a salad of apple, nuts, olives before sleep.

12.06.14

Day 3: Granada - Seville

Just the two cities yesterday. Breakfasted on the terrace overlocking much of the town and in turn being overlooked by the Alhambra. It was towards the that we then set off in the shaded hear up the hills of the Albaicín. This was a wonderful walk. A new wonder around every corner. Constant changes of perspective and colours of buildings eventually emerging at the river to then climb up to the Generalife gardens and palace. Had nice conversations with K about London and Sheffield while walking among cypress trees and fountains. A final walk into town to glimpse the cathedral and Capilla Real, both of which somewhat paled in relation to their Islamic brother son the hill. Granada is hugely appealing and will certainly draw me back. The goodbye took place as we walked back up the hill to the car, slowly slipping out of the town just as we had arrived.

The drive to Saville was fairly un eventful. Caught up with Laurie’s opinions and epitaphs. We arrived and parked with some difficulty but close the apartments on the edge of Triana. The is the area LL describes so vividly so it was a boon to discover we are staying here. The apartment is long, thin, with one central room 3 floors up overlooking a road. Our first night was somewhat inadvertently spent getting quite drunk first on the flat then in bars close and the further into the city. Had a not quite stand-up argument about working hours. It was heated but in love or rather with love. The night ended with expensive brandy and a slow walk home to sleep, after talking until 4:00.

19.10.17

Day 1: London - Bordeaux

The first morning. Awake with a mild hangover, unsurprisingly as spent much money and drank much booze last night. Are staying in a nice apartment in the north of Bordeaux, a light industrial area about 45 mins walk to the centre. This afternoon we are off to Spain en train.

Last night was fun, if eventful for the wrong reasons. We arrived in the city around 5 or 6 having been slightly delayed but consoled by beers at Luton airport. After a long bus journey and a brief meeting with our proprietress we visited the aparto before walking back into town along the river. This was a lovely walk punctuated by a few stops for wine and food. The river bent towards us and widened, very wide at its widest. The prom was well used and intermittently zoned with shops, and bars. All sympathetically done. Dinner was galette and a salad outside a bar with a cave like interior just next to the Hotel de Ville. From here we went searching for more wine settling on cocktails and some new English pals who we later played pool with and then grew tired of.

K had a blighted evening. At the restaurant his card got destroyed by the card reader. Somehow. Later, far more painfully his phone was stolen almost certainly by a guy who was in the last bar. We think he must have handed it off to a mate and then had deniability. All quite horrible and bizarre. A sour note to finish the night on. The walk home was intermittently biter and released. We are now all buttered up and about to leave for a few hours here before the trip south.

20.10.17

Day 2: Bordeaux - Donistia

The second morning now, in the second aparto, second city. Enjoy toast, jam, apples and coffee looking out over the bay of San Sebastian. Yesterday was mainly spent travelling. We left our northern flat and took trams south to Gare Saint John to sus trains and tickets to Spain. Ended up with about 4 hours in hand which we used to wander around saint Michel, through a closing market, some arcades and finally to eat some Italian good before rushing an uber to the station. Enjoyed the Bordeaux visit though it feels a lot like a city for the rich. Perhaps this is just a facet of its architecture and the particular spots we visited.

The journey south was lovely, through pine woods to the coast at Biarritz – on a double decker French train. Spoke of the Cruel Optimism project, London pubs, Midnight clubs of the past and the future. Bands to be formed. Read of Urras, slept a little before arriving at Hendaye and bolted onto the second commuter train – which wound its way along the coast armed with z’s and x’s into Donistia.

The arrival was met with a cool breeze and a blue sky. We walked through the late C19th streets to the sea. This town. It’s so beautiful.

Walking along the prom we soon arrived at our apartment overlooking the harbour with incredible views across the bay. After leaving our bags we walked away from the bay round the head talking of the sublime and sympathetic modernist architecture. Walking back to the old town after stopping for a few beers, we ate in a loud bright cafeteria style bar with hidden bacon under every courgette. We finished the night on the terrace outside a bar with a bottle of red wine looking across the bay at the sea before retiring for sleep and a full Donistian day.

21.10.17

Day 3: Donistia

Woke a little later than planned but just fine really, ate some of the food provided but also an extra bag. Planning to walk round the bay and up the hill we did just that. Barefoot and overlayered we funicualted up to the top of a hill described by an acquaintance of K’s as “black mirror cool” but really simply an old fun fair with an even older observation tower. Ascending the slowly taking in a n old photography exhibition and accompanied by a soundtrack of 90s pop coming from precariously hung speakers. Reaching the roof and incredible vistas we sat, drank the rest of last nights wine and marvelled for the best part of an hour. Upon descending we saw kittens and spoke of sexism before walking back round the quay and briefly home to change then out again to swim and picnic on the beach.
